## Purpose

This document turns the SEO audit's thin-content recommendation into an actionable follow-up. It defines the triage rules and records the first batch of archive URLs that should be reviewed.

## Triage Rules

Keep:
- Original post has clear first-hand experience, useful screenshots, or still earns internal links/backlinks.
- Word count is low but the page still answers a focused query better than a redirect target would.
- The URL has historical value and should stay indexable once the title, description, and internal linking are improved.

Improve or merge:
- Post is roughly 75-400 words and has a strong topic that could support a fuller evergreen article.
- Post overlaps heavily with a stronger article and should be merged into a consolidated replacement with a 301 redirect.
- Post has a promising thesis but reads like a link post or stub.

Deindex or redirect:
- Post is under 75 words and provides little beyond a link, quote, or reaction.
- Post has no unique media, no meaningful internal-link role, and no obvious evergreen query intent.
- Redirect only when there is a clearly better destination; otherwise keep the URL live and mark it `noindex` during cleanup.
